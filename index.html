Higher Education ERM Dashboard

A synthetic COSO-aligned enterprise risk management model using RCSA methodology to illustrate how higher education institutions can identify, assess, monitor, and report financial, compliance, and operational risks.

Executive Summary

This project demonstrates a practical ERM approach for higher education using a centralized risk register, risk scoring, control evaluation, and dashboard-style reporting. The model is built with synthetic data and reflects common institutional risks such as financial aid errors, budget variance, donor concentration, grants compliance, and reporting obligations.

Methodology

COSO ERM Framework

COSO provides the overall governance structure for the model by connecting institutional objectives, risk oversight, monitoring, and reporting.

RCSA Method

Risk and Control Self-Assessment is used as the working assessment method. Each risk is documented, scored for likelihood and impact, linked to key controls, and evaluated for residual exposure after considering control effectiveness.

Scoring Logic

Inherent risk is calculated as likelihood × impact. Residual risk is then adjusted based on control strength. This creates a simple but credible model for prioritizing management attention and mitigation planning.

Dashboard Purpose

The dashboard is designed to support executive and committee-level reporting by highlighting where residual risk remains elevated, where controls may need redesign, and where trend indicators suggest emerging exposure. In a live implementation, this section would include a risk heat map, top risk ranking, KRI trend views, and department-level summaries.

Portfolio Relevance

This project demonstrates how governance, compliance, and data analysis can be translated into a structured ERM model. It is intended as a portfolio example for roles related to enterprise risk management, GRC, higher education compliance, internal controls, audit analytics, and operational risk.
